Liver Disease

Liver disease refers to any condition that affects the liver's structure or function, leading to impairment in its ability to perform vital functions in the body. There are many types of liver diseases, ranging from relatively benign conditions to life-threatening illnesses. Some common types of liver disease include.

Read More

Hepatitis

Inflammation of the liver, often caused by viral infections (hepatitis A, B, C, etc.), alcohol abuse, or autoimmune diseases.

Read More

Nonalcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)

Accumulation of fat in the liver, often associated with obesity, diabetes, insulin resistance, and high blood fat levels.

Read More

Cirrhosis:

Scarring of the liver tissue, usually as a result of long-term liver damage from conditions such as chronic alcoholism, hepatitis B or C, or nonalcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD).

Alcoholic Liver Disease:

Damage to the liver due to excessive alcohol consumption, including fatty liver, alcoholic hepatitis, and cirrhosis.

Liver Cancer:

Malignant tumors that originate in the liver (primary liver cancer) or spread to the liver from other parts of the body (metastatic liver cancer).

Symptoms of Liver Disease:

Symptoms of liver disease can vary depending on the specific condition and its severity but may include fatigue, weakness, jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes), abdominal pain and swelling, nausea, vomiting, and loss of appetite.

Treatment for Liver Disease:

Treatment for liver disease depends on the underlying cause and may include lifestyle changes, medications, or in severe cases, liver transplantation.

Early Detection & Management of Liver Disease

Early detection and management of liver disease are crucial for preventing complications and improving outcomes. Regular medical check-ups and screening tests can help detect liver disease in its early stages when treatment is most effective.
